# 10 Underground Rappers for a Study Playlist

Lucius P. Thundercat is a Canadian hip-hop rapper and vocalist from Montréal whose melodic, pop-forward sound across R&B rap, trap, and lofi makes him a natural fit for study sessions. But he's far from alone in the underground rap space where intelligent production and introspective bars thrive without the hype machine. Here are 10 independent rappers whose catalogs deliver the focus and replay value that makes for a genuinely useful study playlist.

## Why These Artists Work for Studying

Underground rap thrives in the space between mainstream trap's hi-def maximalism and lo-fi hip-hop's deliberate emptiness. The artists above share a discipline: bars that don't demand your full attention, production that breathes instead of overwhelms, and a commitment to the song over the moment. ## Building Your Own List

When scouting study-rap, look for: conversational flow (not aggressive delivery), production that sits behind the vocals (not on top), and artists who value repetition and space over constant novelty. Lucius P. Thundercat's catalog, particularly his feature-driven releases, sits squarely in this lane.
